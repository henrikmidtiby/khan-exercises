The general solution to the differential equation is:

y(t) = A \cdot \sin \left( B \cdot t \right) + C \cdot \cos \left( B \cdot t \right)

From this the value of B is found to be B.

Insertion of the initial function value yields (t = 0 and y(0) = y0val):

y(0) = A \cdot \sin \left( B \cdot 0 \right) + C \cdot \cos \left( B \cdot 0 \right)

y0val = A \cdot \sin(0) + C \cdot \cos(0)

y0val = C

Insertion of the initial function derivative yields (t = 0 and y'(0) = dy0val):

y'(0) = B \cdot A \cdot \cos \left( B \cdot 0 \right) - B \cdot C \cdot \sin \left( B \cdot 0 \right)

dy0val = B \cdot A \cdot \cos(0) - B \cdot C \cdot \sin(0)

dy0val = B \cdot A

A = dy0val / B
